# SE_01: Discovery Script for Property Managers

**Script Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Purpose:** Discovery script for sales conversations with property managers. Identifies pain points, scope, frequency, and decision process.

---

## Opening

**Introduction:**

**Purpose:**
"I'm calling to understand your needs, not to sell you anything today. I'd like to ask a few questions about your current cleaning setup."

---

## Current Vendor Assessment

### Current Vendor

**Question 1:** "Who handles your common area cleaning right now?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "How long have you been with them?"
- "What's working well?"
- "What's not working well?"

**Listen for:**
- Pain points (quality, reliability, communication)
- Satisfaction level (happy, neutral, frustrated)
- Contract status (when does it expire?)

---

### Pain Points

**Question 2:** "What are your biggest challenges with your current cleaning?"

**Common pain points to probe:**
- **Quality issues:** "Are there areas that consistently aren't cleaned well?"
- **Reliability:** "Do they show up consistently? Any missed visits?"
- **Communication:** "How easy is it to get in touch with them? Do they respond quickly?"
- **Pricing:** "Are you comfortable with the pricing? Any surprise charges?"
- **Flexibility:** "Can they handle add-ons or special requests?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific examples of problems
- Frequency of issues
- Impact on residents/board

---

## Scope and Frequency

### Current Scope

**Question 3:** "What areas do they clean? Walk me through what's included."

**Probe for:**
- Lobby
- Hallways
- Stairwells
- Elevators
- Garbage rooms
- Glass doors
- Washrooms
- Other areas

**Question 4:** "Are there areas they don't clean that you'd like cleaned?"

**Listen for:**
- Gaps in current service
- Areas that need more attention
- Add-on opportunities

---

### Frequency

**Question 5:** "How often do they clean? What's the schedule?"

**Probe for:**
- Days per week (2x, 3x, 5x, daily)
- Service windows (morning, afternoon, evening)
- Seasonal variations

**Question 6:** "Is that frequency working, or would you prefer more or less?"

**Listen for:**
- Satisfaction with frequency
- Desired changes
- Budget constraints

---

## Decision Process

### Decision Makers

**Question 7:** "Who's involved in making decisions about cleaning services?"

**Probe for:**
- Property manager (day-to-day)
- Board of directors (approval)
- Management company (oversight)
- Other stakeholders

**Question 8:** "What's the decision process? Do you make recommendations, or does the board decide?"

**Listen for:**
- Decision-making authority
- Approval process
- Timeline for decisions

---

### Contract Status

**Question 9:** "When does your current contract expire? Are you locked in, or can you make changes?"

**Listen for:**
- Contract end date
- Renewal timeline
- Termination clauses
- Opportunity timing

---

### Budget

**Question 10:** "What's your current monthly cleaning budget? Are you looking to stay within that, or is there flexibility?"

**Listen for:**
- Current spend
- Budget constraints
- Willingness to pay more for better service
- Cost sensitivity

---

## Quality and Standards

### Quality Expectations

**Question 11:** "What does 'good cleaning' look like to you? What are your quality standards?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific expectations
- Areas of focus
- Quality metrics (if any)

**Question 12:** "How do you currently verify quality? Do you inspect, or rely on resident feedback?"

**Listen for:**
- Inspection process
- Feedback mechanisms
- Quality assurance methods

---

### Resident Feedback

**Question 13:** "What kind of feedback do you get from residents about cleaning?"

**Listen for:**
- Positive feedback
- Complaints
- Common issues
- Resident expectations

---

## Add-Ons and Special Requests

**Question 14:** "Do you ever need add-on services? Guest suite cleaning, elevator move cleans, deep cleans, bio cleanup?"

**Listen for:**
- Current add-on needs
- Frequency of add-ons
- Current provider's ability to handle add-ons

**Question 15:** "What about light maintenance? Bulb replacements, minor repairs, that kind of thing?"

**Listen for:**
- Maintenance needs
- Current provider's maintenance capabilities
- Interest in bundled services

---

## Access and Site Rules

**Question 16:** "How do cleaners access the building? Keys, FOBs, codes?"

**Listen for:**
- Access method
- Credential management
- Security concerns

**Question 17:** "Are there any special site rules? Parking, service windows, noise restrictions, concierge check-in?"

**Listen for:**
- Site-specific requirements
- Compliance needs
- Operational constraints

---

## Next Steps

### Interest Level

**Question 18:** "Based on what we've discussed, would you be open to a proposal? What would that process look like?"

**Listen for:**
- Interest level
- Decision timeline
- Proposal requirements
- Next steps

---

### Site Walk

**Question 19:** "Would it be helpful if I did a site walk to see the space and provide a more accurate quote?"

**Listen for:**
- Willingness to schedule site walk
- Availability
- Site walk requirements

---

## Closing

**Summary:**
"Thank you for your time. Based on what you've shared, I think we might be a good fit. Here's what I'll do next: [NEXT STEPS]."

**Follow-up:**
- Send follow-up email summarizing conversation
- Schedule site walk (if interested)
- Prepare proposal (if appropriate)
- Set follow-up call date
