# POL_02: Health Safety and PPE

**Policy Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define health and safety requirements, PPE usage, incident reporting, and stop-work authority. Ensures worker safety and compliance.

---

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### Required PPE

**Standard cleaning:**
- **Gloves:** Disposable or reusable gloves for cleaning tasks
- **Closed-toe shoes:** Non-slip, protective footwear
- **Appropriate clothing:** Work-appropriate, no loose clothing

**Garbage room cleaning:**
- **Gloves:** Heavy-duty gloves (cut-resistant if handling sharp objects)
- **Mask:** N95 or equivalent (for odor and particulate protection)
- **Closed-toe shoes:** Non-slip, protective footwear

**Washroom cleaning:**
- **Gloves:** Disposable gloves (changed between tasks)
- **Closed-toe shoes:** Non-slip, protective footwear

**Bio cleanup (trained workers only):**
- **Gloves:** Heavy-duty, cut-resistant
- **Mask:** N95 or equivalent
- **Protective suit:** Disposable coverall (if needed)
- **Eye protection:** Safety glasses or face shield (if needed)

---

### PPE Usage

**Before starting work:**
- [ ] Verify PPE available
- [ ] Don appropriate PPE for task
- [ ] Verify PPE fits properly

**During work:**
- Change gloves between tasks (especially washroom â†’ other areas)
- Replace damaged PPE immediately
- Do not reuse disposable PPE

**After work:**
- Remove PPE properly (gloves inside-out, mask last)
- Dispose of disposable PPE properly
- Clean reusable PPE (gloves, shoes)

## Chemical Safety

### Chemical Handling

**Before using chemicals:**
- [ ] Read product label and safety data sheet (SDS)
- [ ] Verify chemical appropriate for surface type
- [ ] Verify proper dilution (if needed)
- [ ] Verify ventilation adequate

**During use:**
- Use chemicals per manufacturer instructions
- Never mix chemicals (unless manufacturer-approved)
- Use appropriate PPE
- Avoid contact with skin and eyes

**Storage:**
- Store chemicals in original containers
- Label containers clearly
- Store away from heat and direct sunlight
- Keep out of reach of residents/visitors

---

### Chemical-Specific Guidelines

**Glass cleaner:**
- Use in well-ventilated areas
- Avoid contact with eyes
- Use gloves if skin sensitive

**Disinfectants:**
- Allow contact time per product instructions
- Use gloves
- Avoid mixing with other chemicals

**Floor cleaners:**
- Use appropriate cleaner for floor type (tile, hardwood, carpet)
- Avoid excess water on hardwood
- Use non-slip footwear

**Deodorizers:**
- Use in well-ventilated areas
- Avoid overuse (can cause respiratory irritation)

---

## Slip and Fall Prevention

### Prevention Measures

**Before cleaning:**
- [ ] Check for wet or slippery surfaces
- [ ] Place "Wet Floor" signage if needed
- [ ] Verify non-slip footwear worn

**During cleaning:**
- Mop in sections (don't leave entire floor wet)
- Dry mop after wet mopping (if needed)
- Remove "Wet Floor" signage when dry

**After cleaning:**
- Verify floor dry before leaving area
- Remove signage
- Report any persistent slippery surfaces to admin

---

### If Slip/Fall Occurs

**Step 1:** Assess injury:
- Minor (bruise, scrape): Document, continue if safe
- Serious (pain, inability to move): **STOP WORK**, seek medical attention

**Step 2:** Document incident:
- Type: SAFETY
- Description: What happened, where, when
- Photos: If safe to take
- Report immediately to admin

**Step 3:** Admin coordinates:
- Medical attention if needed
- Workers' compensation if applicable
- Site contact notified if needed

---

## Incident Reporting

### Reportable Incidents

**Report immediately:**

1. **Safety hazards:**
   - Exposed wires
   - Broken glass or sharp objects
   - Slippery surfaces (water leaks, spills)
   - Structural damage

2. **Injuries:**
   - Worker injury (slip, fall, cut, chemical exposure)
   - Resident/visitor injury (if related to cleaning)

3. **Biohazards:**
   - Blood or bodily fluids
   - Needles or sharps
   - Dead animals

4. **Property damage:**
   - Damage caused by worker
   - Damage discovered (water leaks, broken fixtures)

**How to report:**
- Via portal: Create incident report (type: SAFETY / PROPERTY_DAMAGE / BIOHAZARD)
- Contact admin immediately if urgent
- Document with photos if safe

## Stop-Work Authority

### When to Stop Work

**Workers have stop-work authority:**

- **Safety hazard identified:** STOP WORK immediately
- **Biohazard encountered:** STOP WORK (unless trained and equipped)
- **Chemical spill or exposure:** STOP WORK immediately
- **Injury occurs:** STOP WORK, seek medical attention if needed
- **Unsafe conditions:** STOP WORK until resolved

**No retaliation for exercising stop-work authority.**

---

### Stop-Work Procedure

**Step 1:** Identify unsafe condition  
**Step 2:** **STOP WORK immediately**  
**Step 3:** Document incident:
- Type: SAFETY
- Description: Unsafe condition
- Location: Specific area
- Photos: If safe to take

**Step 4:** Contact admin immediately  
**Step 5:** Wait for admin response:
- Admin coordinates resolution
- Worker does not proceed until safe

---

## Biohazard Procedures

### Biohazard Encounter

**If biohazard encountered:**

1. **STOP WORK immediately**
2. **Do not attempt cleanup** (unless trained and equipped)
3. **Document incident:**
   - Type: BIOHAZARD
   - Description: What encountered, where
   - Photos: If safe to take
4. **Contact admin immediately**
5. **Admin coordinates:**
   - Trained biohazard cleanup crew
   - Or site contact arranges cleanup
6. **Do not proceed** until area cleaned by trained personnel

---

### Biohazard Cleanup (Trained Workers Only)

**If trained and equipped:**

1. **Don appropriate PPE:**
   - Heavy-duty gloves
   - N95 mask
   - Protective suit (if needed)
   - Eye protection (if needed)

2. **Remove biohazard safely:**
   - Use appropriate tools (not bare hands)
   - Place in biohazard bag
   - Seal bag properly

3. **Sanitize area:**
   - Use appropriate sanitizer
   - Allow contact time
   - Verify area clean

4. **Dispose of materials:**
   - Per regulations
   - Proper disposal site
   - Document disposal

5. **Remove PPE properly:**
   - Gloves inside-out
   - Mask last
   - Dispose of disposable PPE
   - Wash hands thoroughly

---

## Needle and Sharps

### If Needle/Sharp Found

**DO NOT handle needles or sharps:**

1. **STOP WORK immediately**
2. **Do not attempt to pick up or move**
3. **Document incident:**
   - Type: BIOHAZARD
   - Description: Needle/sharp found, location
   - Photos: If safe to take
4. **Contact admin immediately**
5. **Admin coordinates:**
   - Trained sharps disposal crew
   - Or site contact arranges disposal
6. **Do not proceed** until sharps removed by trained personnel

---

## Chemical Exposure

### If Chemical Exposure Occurs

**Skin contact:**
- Remove contaminated clothing
- Rinse skin with water (15+ minutes)
- Seek medical attention if irritation persists
- Document incident

**Eye contact:**
- Rinse eyes with water (15+ minutes)
- Seek medical attention immediately
- Document incident

**Inhalation:**
- Move to fresh air
- Seek medical attention if symptoms persist
- Document incident

**Ingestion:**
- Do not induce vomiting
- Seek medical attention immediately
- Bring product label/SDS to medical provider
- Document incident
