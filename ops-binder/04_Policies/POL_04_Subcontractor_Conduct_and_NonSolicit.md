# POL_04: Subcontractor Conduct and Non-Solicit

**Policy Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define subcontractor conduct requirements, non-solicitation rules, and consequences for violations. Protects company relationships and ensures professionalism.

---

## Professional Conduct Requirements

### Required Conduct

**Workers must:**

1. **Be professional:**
   - Courteous and respectful to residents and visitors
   - Punctual (arrive within service window)
   - Complete work per checklist and SOPs
   - Follow site rules and instructions

2. **Maintain appearance:**
   - Clean, work-appropriate clothing
   - Company identification (if provided)
   - Professional demeanor

3. **Communicate appropriately:**
   - Only with admin (not directly with clients)
   - Professional language
   - Report issues via portal or admin contact

4. **Follow procedures:**
   - Complete checklists per SOPs
   - Take required photos
   - Submit completions on time
   - Follow safety procedures

---

## Prohibited Conduct

### Strictly Prohibited

**Workers MUST NOT:**

1. **Solicit residents:**
   - No soliciting residents for personal services
   - No soliciting residents for competing business
   - No handing out business cards or contact information
   - No discussing personal services or side work

2. **Discuss pricing:**
   - No discussing company rates with residents
   - No discussing pricing with clients
   - No negotiating rates (admin only)

3. **Accept gifts:**
   - No accepting tips, gifts, or favors from residents
   - No accepting gifts from clients
   - Politely decline: "Thank you, but I cannot accept tips/gifts."

4. **Use social media:**
   - No posting photos of sites or residents
   - No posting comments about sites or clients
   - No identifying company clients on social media

5. **Enter resident units:**
   - No entering units (even if invited)
   - No cleaning units (out of scope)
   - No accepting unit work (direct to property management)

6. **Share contact information:**
   - No sharing personal phone/email with residents
   - No sharing personal contact with clients
   - All communication via admin

---

## Non-Solicitation and Non-Circumvention

### Non-Solicitation Period

**Duration:** 24 months after termination of subcontractor agreement

**Prohibited during non-solicitation period:**

1. **Client solicitation:**
   - No soliciting clients introduced by company
   - No contacting clients directly
   - No offering competing services to company clients

2. **Site solicitation:**
   - No soliciting sites where worker performed services
   - No contacting property management directly
   - No offering competing services to company sites

3. **Worker solicitation:**
   - No soliciting company workers to leave
   - No recruiting company workers for competing business

---

### Non-Circumvention

**Prohibited:**

- **Circumventing company:** No going around company to contact clients directly
- **Direct relationships:** No establishing direct relationships with clients
- **Competing services:** No offering competing services to company clients

**Exception:** Worker may work for clients not introduced by company (must prove no company introduction).

---

## Remedies for Breach

### Injunctive Relief

**Company may seek:**

- **Injunction:** Court order preventing solicitation/circumvention
- **Immediate enforcement:** No waiting period
- **Geographic scope:** Windsor–Essex and surrounding areas

---

### Liquidated Damages

**Per breach:**

- **Per client/site:** Reasonable amount per client/site solicited
- **Genuine pre-estimate:** Amount reflects genuine loss estimate
- **Not penalty:** Enforceable as liquidated damages (not penalty)

**Calculation:**
- Based on lost revenue per client/site
- Documented in subcontractor agreement
- Reasonable and enforceable

**Example:** If worker solicits Client A, liquidated damages = [amount] per client/site solicited.

---

### Termination

**Immediate termination for:**

1. **Solicitation breach:**
   - Soliciting residents or clients
   - Establishing direct relationships
   - Violating non-solicitation period

2. **Repeated conduct violations:**
   - Multiple warnings for conduct issues
   - Pattern of unprofessional behavior

3. **Gross misconduct:**
   - Theft or property damage
   - Safety violations
   - Legal violations

---

## Violation Consequences

### First Offense

**Warning and retraining:**
- Written warning
- Retraining on conduct policy
- Documented in worker record
- No removal from site (unless serious)

---

### Second Offense

**Removal from site:**
- Worker removed from specific site
- Alternative worker assigned
- Documented in audit log
- May affect future assignments

---

### Third Offense

**Termination:**
- Subcontractor agreement terminated
- Worker removed from all sites
- Non-solicitation period begins (24 months)
- Legal action if solicitation breach

---

### Solicitation Breach

**Immediate termination + legal action:**
- Termination effective immediately
- Injunctive relief sought
- Liquidated damages claimed
- Non-solicitation period enforced (24 months)

---

## Reporting Violations

### If Worker Observes Violation

**Report immediately:**

1. **Document violation:**
   - What happened
   - When and where
   - Who was involved
   - Any evidence (photos, witnesses)

2. **Report to admin:**
   - Via portal message
   - Or phone if urgent
   - Admin investigates

3. **Admin action:**
   - Investigates report
   - Takes appropriate action
   - Documents in audit log

---

### If Resident Reports Violation

**Admin procedure:**

1. **Receive complaint:**
   - From property management or resident
   - Document complaint details

2. **Investigate:**
   - Review worker history
   - Interview worker if needed
   - Review evidence

3. **Take action:**
   - Warning, removal, or termination
   - Document in audit log
   - Notify property management

---

## Decision Tree: Common Scenarios

### Scenario: Resident Offers Tip
→ **Politely decline:** "Thank you, but I cannot accept tips." → **Continue work** → **Report if persistent**

### Scenario: Resident Asks for Personal Cleaning Services
→ **Politely decline:** "I only clean common areas. Please contact property management for unit cleaning." → **Do not provide contact information** → **Report if solicitation attempt**

### Scenario: Resident Complains About Company Rates
→ **Do not discuss pricing** → **Direct to property management:** "Please contact property management with any pricing questions." → **Document in notes**

### Scenario: Another Worker Soliciting Residents
→ **Report immediately to admin** → **Document via incident report** → **Admin investigates and takes action**
